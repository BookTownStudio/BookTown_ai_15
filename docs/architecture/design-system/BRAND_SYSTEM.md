# BookTown Brand System

Status: Foundation architecture.

## Brand Architecture

BookTown is the master brand for a literary intelligence platform. All product modules inherit from the master brand and may express local personality only through governed sub-brand rules.

Primary product domains:

- Read
- Write
- Discover
- Social
- MatchMaker
- Agents
- Marketplace
- Admin

## Brand Pillars

1. Literary intelligence: recommendations, agents, and interpretation should feel knowledgeable and grounded.
2. Reader trust: progress, shelves, annotations, and ownership must feel stable.
3. Creative authorship: writers should feel in control of their manuscripts and publishing path.
4. Cultural bilingualism: Arabic and English are equal first-class experiences.
5. Community depth: social surfaces exist for books, quotes, authors, shelves, and venues.

## Visual Identity Principles

The visual identity should combine:

- Structured dark and light surfaces.
- Carefully governed literary paper tones for reading.
- A restrained sky/accent system for action and intelligence.
- Typographic contrast between utility UI and longform text.
- Consistent iconography that supports scanning.

Brand expression must come from composition, typography, continuity, and thoughtful state design, not decorative gradients or excessive illustration.

## Logo Philosophy

The BookTown logo is a brand anchor, not a general UI decoration. It should appear in identity moments such as login, install, launch, public brand surfaces, and official exports.

Logo governance must define:

- Primary lockup.
- Compact mark.
- Monochrome variant.
- Light background variant.
- Dark background variant.
- Minimum clear space.
- Minimum rendered size.
- Forbidden transformations.

## Product Sub-Brand Strategy

Sub-brands are functional modes, not independent brands.

| Domain | Expression |
| --- | --- |
| Read | Quiet, spacious, paper/ink aware |
| Write | Focused, stable, tool-oriented |
| Discover | Exploratory, guided, visually rich but bounded |
| Social | Conversational, book-centered, not engagement-heavy |
| MatchMaker | Intelligent, transparent, preference-aware |
| Agents | Useful literary companions with consistent system identity |
| Marketplace | Trustworthy, transactional, restrained |
| Admin | Dense, operational, explicit state and authority |

## Brand Consistency Rules

1. The master brand owns typography, color semantics, icon rules, and motion principles.
2. Product domains may specialize density and layout, not invent new palettes.
3. Reader paper themes are governed brand extensions.
4. Partner and marketplace surfaces must not override core accessibility or navigation patterns.
5. Brand assets require documented source, variants, and usage constraints.

## Future Governance

Brand changes require design-system review when they affect:

- Logo files or usage.
- Primary/accent color roles.
- Product domain identities.
- Typography families or weights.
- Public marketing-to-product continuity.
- Native app identity.
