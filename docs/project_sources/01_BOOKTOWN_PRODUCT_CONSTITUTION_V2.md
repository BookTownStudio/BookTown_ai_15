# BookTown Product Constitution v2

## Authority Scope

This document is the product authority for what BookTown is, why it exists, what it must not become, and how product decisions should align with the locked architecture foundations.

## Product Identity

BookTown is a Literary Intelligence Platform.

BookTown is not a bookstore. BookTown is not a reading tracker. BookTown is not a generic social network. BookTown is not a recommendation wrapper. BookTown is not an LLM interface.

BookTown exists to help people read, write, discover, remember, interpret, and develop through literature. Its product systems are built around literary truth, reader identity, author identity, meaning, relationships, and durable cultural context.

## Product Philosophy

BookTown treats literature as a structured, living domain of knowledge. Works, Authors, Quotes, Themes, Concepts, Movements, Periods, Traditions, and literary relationships are not merely UI labels. They are governed objects or governed relationships with authority boundaries.

Product surfaces must make authority usable without weakening it. Search, Discovery, Reader, Public Web, Publishing, MatchMaker, Home, Shelves, Quotes, Reviews, Messaging, and Notifications are consumers of canonical authority where literary truth is involved.

## Core Principles

| Principle | Product Rule |
|---|---|
| Literary truth first | Product convenience must not redefine canonical Work, Author, Meaning, or Graph truth. |
| Work-centric catalog | Search and discovery center on Works while preserving Edition and Manifestation boundaries. |
| Meaning requires authority | Theme and Concept must come from Meaning Unit Authority, not raw tags, AI phrases, provider labels, or search behavior. |
| Relationships require evidence | Literary Graph relationships require eligible nodes, provenance, confidence, lifecycle, and authority acceptance. |
| Intelligence consumes truth | MatchMaker and AI generate derived intelligence, not canonical identity, meaning, or graph truth. |
| Projection is not authority | Cards, summaries, feeds, public pages, search indexes, and recommendation outputs are projections. |
| Users are not literary entities | User identity connects through interactions and Identity Graph, not `LiteraryEntityRef`. |

## Product Boundaries

| Product Area | Boundary |
|---|---|
| Search | Finds and ranks authority-backed records; cannot create catalog, author, meaning, or graph truth. |
| Discovery | Surfaces editorial and derived paths; cannot certify literary relationships. |
| Reader | Owns reading state and reader media surfaces; cannot redefine Work, Edition, or Manifestation identity. |
| Publishing | Owns publishing workflows and publications; Work/Author authority must route through foundations. |
| Public Web | Exposes approved public projections; cannot become source of truth. |
| MatchMaker | Produces literary intelligence outputs; cannot create canonical truth. |
| Social/Messaging | Supports communication and attachments; cannot turn interaction into literary authority. |

## Authority Hierarchy

1. Locked foundations govern identity, meaning, and relationships.
2. Master documents route domain authority.
3. Project Source canons consolidate retrieval memory.
4. Product surfaces consume authority through typed contracts and projections.
5. Legacy documents are historical when they conflict with locked doctrine.

## Strategic Vision

BookTown should become the trusted platform for literary understanding: a place where readers and writers can move from finding books to understanding Works, Authors, Ideas, Traditions, and their own literary identity.

The product strategy is to build durable authority first, then scale Search, Reader, Discovery, MatchMaker, Public Web, and Publishing on top of that foundation.
